Section 01

Executive Summary

A high-level overview of findings, critical issues, and strategic recommendations.

⚙
Overview

This report presents a comprehensive SEO (Search Engine Optimization) and AEO (AI Engine Optimization) audit of properavionix.in, the official website of Proper Avionix Private Limited — an Indian Urban Air Mobility (UAM) and eVTOL company based in Hyderabad, Telangana.


The website is currently built on the Wix platform, which introduces significant technical limitations that affect search visibility, performance, and the ability to compete effectively in both traditional search and emerging AI-powered search engines.


The audit evaluated the website across 5 core dimensions: Technical SEO, On-Page Content, E-E-A-T Signals, AEO Readiness, and Competitive Positioning. Each dimension was scored independently to provide a clear, actionable picture of where the website stands and where improvements will yield the highest return.

⚠ Critical Findings

  • No structured data (Schema markup) — The website has zero JSON-LD schema implementation. This means search engines and AI engines cannot reliably extract company information, product details, or FAQ content.
  • Missing or weak meta tags — Title tags are generic ("Home | Proper Avionix"), meta descriptions appear absent, and H1 tags lack target keywords.
  • Zero AEO readiness — Content is not structured for AI engine citation. No semantic triples, no FAQ schema, no entity-first writing patterns. The brand is invisible to ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews.
  • Multiple pages timing out — Several key pages (Programs, Technology & Team, Be Familiarized) failed to load during our analysis, indicating severe performance or configuration issues.
  • No press coverage or backlinks detected — The brand has virtually no external authority signals, severely limiting E-E-A-T credibility.

✓ Opportunities & Strengths

  • First-mover advantage — Proper Avionix operates in the rapidly growing Indian UAM market (projected $23.47 billion globally by 2030). Competitors like Sarla Aviation, ePlane Company, and global players are investing heavily in SEO. Early optimization creates lasting advantage.
  • Unique product portfolio — Nimbus Hop (passenger), Defense Hex (military), Air Medic (healthcare), and Carbon Capture drones provide rich content opportunities across multiple high-value verticals.
  • HTTPS is enabled — The site is served over HTTPS, which is a baseline trust and ranking signal.
  • robots.txt and sitemap configured — Basic crawling infrastructure exists and can be optimized further.

Section 03

Technical SEO Analysis

Evaluation of site architecture, crawlability, performance, and technical infrastructure.

⚠
Platform Assessment — Wix

The website is built on Wix, a website builder platform. While Wix has improved its SEO capabilities in recent years, it still imposes significant technical limitations compared to custom-built or CMS-based solutions:


  • ✗ Limited server-side rendering control — Wix handles rendering internally, resulting in JavaScript-heavy page loads that can delay content discovery by search engine crawlers.
  • ✗ Restricted schema markup — Wix does not natively support custom JSON-LD structured data beyond basic page types. This severely limits the ability to implement Organization, Product, FAQ, and Article schema.
  • ✗ Auto-generated sitemap — The sitemap is auto-generated and could not be fetched successfully during testing, indicating potential configuration or accessibility issues.
  • ⚠ Limited page speed optimization — Wix controls CDN caching, image optimization, and code splitting. Customization options are minimal, which affects Core Web Vitals performance.
  • ✓ HTTPS enabled — The site is served over HTTPS, meeting baseline security requirements for search rankings.
  • ✓ robots.txt exists — A robots.txt file is present with appropriate directives, though it is auto-generated by Wix.

Section 08

Prioritized Recommendations

Actionable recommendations ordered by impact and urgency.

■ Critical Priority — Immediate Action Required

1. Migrate Off Wix to a Custom-Built or Headless CMS Platform
CRITICAL
Wix imposes severe technical SEO and AEO limitations that cannot be overcome through content changes alone. Migrate to a modern framework (Next.js, Astro, or WordPress with headless CMS) that provides full control over metadata, schema markup, server-side rendering, page speed, and URL structure. This is the single highest-impact change and the foundation for all other recommendations.
Impact: Very High (enables all other optimizations)
Effort: High (4-8 weeks)
Timeline: 0-60 days
2. Fix Broken / Timing-Out Pages
CRITICAL
Three key pages (Programs, Technology & Team, Be Familiarized) are completely inaccessible. These pages likely contain important content about the company's capabilities, team credentials, and programs. Fix immediately — any page that doesn't load is effectively invisible to both search engines and users. Diagnose whether the issue is Wix configuration, heavy media files, or broken page elements.
Impact: Very High (~43% of site is down)
Effort: Low-Medium
Timeline: Immediately
3. Implement Structured Data (Schema Markup) Across All Pages
CRITICAL
Deploy JSON-LD schema markup on every page. At minimum, implement: Organization (company info, logo, contact), Product (for Nimbus Hop, Defense Hex, Air Medic), FAQPage (after adding FAQ sections), and Article (for any long-form content or blog posts). This is the single fastest way to improve both SEO rich results and AEO citation probability.
Impact: Very High (enables rich results + AI extraction)
Effort: Medium
Timeline: 0-30 days

■ High Priority — Within 30 Days

4. Rewrite All Title Tags and Meta Descriptions
HIGH
Replace generic titles ("Home | Proper Avionix") with keyword-rich, compelling titles for every page. Each title should be 50-60 characters with the primary keyword at the front. Write unique meta descriptions (150-160 characters) for each page that include a clear value proposition and call to action.
Impact: High (directly affects CTR and rankings)
Effort: Low
Timeline: 1-2 weeks
5. Restructure All Content Using Semantic Triples and AEO Patterns
HIGH
Rewrite website copy to follow the Feature → How → Outcome pattern. Every paragraph should contain 2-4 clear semantic triples (Subject → Verb → Object). Replace vague buzzwords with specific, quantifiable claims. Name entities (Nimbus Hop, Defense Hex, Air Medic POD) explicitly and consistently across all pages.
Impact: High (enables AI citation)
Effort: Medium
Timeline: 2-4 weeks
6. Fix All Grammar, Spelling, and Content Quality Issues
HIGH
Professional credibility is undermined by spelling and grammar errors throughout the site. Conduct a complete editorial review and rewrite. Remove ALL CAPS headings (accessibility issue). Replace jargon-heavy copy with clear, confident language that serves both technical audiences and potential investors.
Impact: High (credibility + E-E-A-T)
Effort: Low
Timeline: 1-2 weeks
7. Add FAQ Sections with Schema Markup
HIGH
Add 3-5 FAQ questions on each major page, addressing common queries about eVTOL technology, safety, pricing, and availability in India. Implement FAQPage schema markup for each. This is one of the highest-ROI AEO tactics — FAQ schema is directly used by AI engines to generate answers.
Impact: High (rich results + AI answers)
Effort: Low
Timeline: 1-2 weeks

■ Medium Priority — Within 60 Days

8. Launch a Content Hub / Blog
MEDIUM
Create a blog or content hub covering topics across all four verticals: civilian UAM, defense, medical, and carbon capture. Publish 2-4 articles per month targeting high-value keywords. Each article should follow AEO content templates (Category Explainer, Use Case, Supporting Blog Post). This builds topical authority and creates internal linking opportunities.
Impact: High (long-term authority building)
Effort: Medium-High (ongoing)
Timeline: 30-60 days to launch
9. Create Dedicated Product Pages for Each Aircraft Variant
MEDIUM
Create individual, keyword-optimized pages for: Nimbus Hop (passenger eVTOL), Defense Hex (military drone), Air Medic POD (medical), and Carbon Capture drone. Each page should include full technical specifications, use cases, comparison tables, and FAQ sections with schema markup. This is critical for both SEO ranking and AI entity recognition.
Impact: High
Effort: Medium
Timeline: 30-60 days
10. Build Backlinks Through PR and Industry Partnerships
MEDIUM
Develop a PR strategy to get coverage in Indian aviation publications, tech media, and defense journals. Target publications like Economic Times, YourStory, Inc42, LiveMint, and defense-specific outlets. Backlinks from authoritative sources are the strongest signal for both traditional SEO rankings and AI engine authority.
Impact: Very High (authority building)
Effort: High (ongoing)
Timeline: Ongoing

■ Additional Recommendations

11. Add Team Credentials and Author Bios
STANDARD
Create detailed team pages with professional bios, credentials, and LinkedIn profiles. Add author attribution to all content. This directly strengthens E-E-A-T signals that both search engines and AI engines use to evaluate content trustworthiness.
12. Optimize All Images with Alt Text and Modern Formats
STANDARD
Add descriptive, keyword-rich alt text to every image. Convert images to WebP format for faster loading. Implement lazy loading for below-the-fold images. This improves accessibility, page speed, and image search visibility.
13. Add Privacy Policy, Terms of Service, and Legal Pages
STANDARD
Add essential legal pages to improve trust signals. Include a privacy policy, terms of service, and cookie policy. These are baseline expectations for any professional website and are factored into trustworthiness assessments.
14. Implement Internal Linking Strategy
STANDARD
Create a deliberate internal linking structure with 3-5 contextual links per page. Link product pages to the Vision page, link use cases to product specs, and link blog articles to product pages. This distributes link equity, aids crawling, and builds topical clusters that AI engines can follow.

Glossary of Key Terms
Term Definition
SEO Search Engine Optimization — the practice of improving a website's visibility in traditional search engine results (Google, Bing).
AEO AI Engine Optimization — optimizing content to be cited and referenced by AI-powered search and answer engines (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews).
E-E-A-T Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness — Google's framework for evaluating content quality and credibility.
Schema Markup Structured data code (JSON-LD format) added to web pages to help search engines understand the content's meaning and context.
Semantic Triples Clear Subject → Verb → Object fact statements that AI engines can reliably extract and cite (e.g., "Nimbus Hop carries 496 kg payload").
Core Web Vitals Google's metrics for measuring real-world user experience: Largest Contentful Paint (loading), Interaction to Next Paint (responsiveness), and Cumulative Layout Shift (visual stability).
Content Clusters A strategic content architecture where a central "pillar" page links to and from related supporting content, building topical authority.
Backlinks Links from external websites pointing to your site. Quality backlinks from authoritative sources are a major ranking factor.
